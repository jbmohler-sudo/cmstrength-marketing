Beyond Static Percentages

Traditional periodization uses fixed percentages: 70% for a 5-rep set, 80% for a 3-rep set, etc. These are one-size-fits-all prescriptions that ignore individual variation, fatigue state, and training history.

CMS rejects this approach. Instead, it uses RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) as the primary metric and calculates actual load dynamically based on your estimated 1RM (e1RM), recent performance, and readiness score.

The Load Calculation Algorithm (4-Step Process)

Step 1: Determine e1RM (Estimated 1 Rep Max)

CMS uses the Brzycki formula to estimate your 1RM based on your last successful heavy lift:

e1RM = Weight ÷ (1.0278 − 0.0278 × Reps)

Example: If you lifted 315 lbs for 3 reps, your e1RM = 315 ÷ (1.0278 − 0.0834) = 315 ÷ 0.9444 = 333.5 lbs

This e1RM is recalculated after every top set logged in the app. It's your current strength ceiling.

Step 2: RPE → Percent Conversion

Your prescribed RPE (e.g., RPE 7) is converted to a percentage of e1RM using a standard table:

RPE 1 Rep 2 Reps 3 Reps 5 Reps
6 85% 82% 80% 76%
7 90% 87% 85% 81%
8 93% 91% 88% 85%
9 97% 95% 92% 90%

Step 3: Calculate Raw Weight

Multiply the RPE-derived percent by your e1RM:

Raw Weight = e1RM × Percent

Example: e1RM = 333.5 lbs, RPE 8 for 3 reps = 88% → 333.5 × 0.88 = 293.5 lbs

Step 4: Round to Nearest 5 lbs

All prescribed weights are rounded to the nearest 5 lbs for loading convenience. 293.5 lbs becomes 295 lbs (or 290 lbs, depending on direction).

Why 5-lb Increments? Most commercial barbells and gym setups use 5-lb plates. Rounding to 5-lb increments ensures your prescribed load is loadable without fractions or improvisation.

Real-Time Recalculation: Backdown Logic

After you complete your top set, the app recalculates the backdown sets based on your actual top set weight, not the prescribed weight.

Example Scenario

Prescribed Top Set: 295 lbs × 3 reps @ RPE 8

What You Actually Lifted: 300 lbs × 3 reps @ RPE 7 (felt easier)

Backdown Prescription (RPE 7): 300 × 0.85 = 255 lbs

The app recalculates your backdowns using the actual 300 lbs, not the prescribed 295 lbs. This ensures backdowns are responsive to real performance, not stale prescriptions.

Movement-Specific History

CMS tracks performance on every exercise independently. Your bench press e1RM is separate from your dumbbell flye e1RM. When you return to an exercise after a layoff, the app remembers your prior peak performance and uses that as a starting point—not a reset to zero.

Key Takeaways

  • Load = RPE → Percent → e1RM × Percent → Round to 5 lbs
  • e1RM updates after every top set based on Brzycki formula
  • Backdowns recalculate in real-time based on actual top set weight
  • Each exercise tracked independently—no cross-movement assumptions
  • All math is transparent and auditable in your session data
