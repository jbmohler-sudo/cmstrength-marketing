The Three Weak Points

Every bench presser has a sticking point—a range where the bar slows or stops. CMS categorizes these into three archetypal weak points:

1. Off-Chest (Bottom Position)

The bar stalls immediately after leaving the chest. You lack the explosive power from the bottom, or your chest/front delts aren't driving hard enough.

Secondary Lift: Spoto Press (paused, 1 inch off chest)

Accessory: Dumbbell Flyes 2×12 (pec activation, range of motion)

2. Mid-Range (Stall Midway)

The bar moves well off the chest, but stalls 2–4 inches up. You lack midrange strength or scapular stability.

Secondary Lift: Pause Bench (2 sec pause midway) or 2″ Board Press

Accessory: Seal Rows 2×10 (scapular stability, back strength)

3. Lockout (Top Position)

The bar moves well until near the top, then stalls. Your triceps and front delts lack lockout strength.

Secondary Lift: Close-Grip Bench Press (triceps emphasis)

Accessory: Skull Crushers 2×10 (triceps isolation, lockout strength)

No Weak Point? If you're balanced across all ranges, CMS defaults to the Larsen Press (a neutral-grip strength builder) with no specific accessory.

The Weak Point Selection Flow

You identify your weak point during onboarding (Week 1 of your first program). CMS asks you to describe where the bar stalls, then maps your answer to one of the three categories.

After the first block, you can reassess at a block gate. Did your off-chest weakness improve? Switch to mid-range focus. This flexibility allows CMS to adapt to your progress.

How CMS Routes Exercises

The weak point directly determines exercise selection in two ways:

1. Secondary Lift Selection (Skill & Strength Blocks)

Your secondary lift rotates based on weak point and block type:

Weak Point Skill Block Secondary Strength Block Secondary
Off-Chest Spoto Press Competition Bench
Mid-Range Pause Bench (2 sec) 2″ Board Press
Lockout Close-Grip Bench Close-Grip Bench

2. Accessory Selection

Accessories are paired with the secondary lift to reinforce weak-point-specific adaptation:

  • Off-chest: Flyes (chest range)
  • Mid-range: Rows (scapular stability)
  • Lockout: Skull Crushers (triceps)

Real-World Example: Off-Chest Case Study

Lifter Profile

Jake, bench press 1RM: 315 lbs. Bar stalls 1–2 inches off the chest.

Week 1 (Skill Block)

  • Main: Bench Press 5×5 @ RPE 6–7
  • Secondary: Spoto Press (paused 1 inch off chest) 4×5 @ RPE 6.5
  • Accessory: Dumbbell Flyes 2×12 @ RPE 5–6

The Spoto Press trains the bottom-range explosiveness. Flyes activate the pecs through a larger range of motion.

Week 5 (Strength Block)

  • Main: Bench Press 4×4 @ RPE 7–8
  • Secondary: Competition Bench (full range) 3×3 @ RPE 7.5
  • Accessory: Dumbbell Flyes 2×12 @ RPE 5–6

By now, Spoto Press weakness has mostly resolved. The program shifts to competition bench at higher intensity, maintaining flyes for pec drive and resilience.

Week 9 (Peak Block)

  • Main: Bench Press 3×3, 1×1 @ RPE 8–9
  • Secondary: Heavy Bench Competition Variation 2×2 @ RPE 8.5
  • Accessory: Dumbbell Flyes 2×8 (heavier weight) @ RPE 6–7

The focus shifts to meet-specific intensity. Off-chest strength is now solid; CMS reinforces it with heavier flyes to maintain pec resilience.

Result

After 13 weeks, Jake's competition bench (bottom-range drive + full-range power) improved from 315 to 330 lbs—a 15-lb PR specifically in the weak point area.

Why This Works

  • Specificity: Each weak point has a unique secondary lift that directly targets the sticking point range
  • Consistency: You address the same weakness every session (secondary lift + accessory), allowing adaptation
  • Data-Driven: Weak point routing is informed by bench press biomechanics research, not guesswork
  • Progressive Overload: As weakness improves, the program shifts focus, preventing plateau

Key Takeaways

  • Three archetypal weak points: off-chest, mid-range, lockout
  • Each weak point has a specific secondary lift + accessory pairing
  • Weak point selection happens at onboarding and can be reassessed at block gates
  • Secondary lifts and accessories are automatically routed based on your weak point
  • Specialization accelerates improvement in your specific sticking point
