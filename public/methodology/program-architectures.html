The Three Paths

CMS offers three distinct program architectures, each optimized for different goals and athlete profiles:

1. Bench Specialization (13 Weeks)

Focus: Maximize bench press 1RM only

Best for: Bench-focused competitors, singles lifters, or those training between meets

Philosophy: Every session is bench-centric. Secondary lifts and accessories exist to support bench press gains, nothing else.

2. Jacked & Strong (12 Weeks)

Focus: Balanced strength + hypertrophy across all lifts

Best for: Accessory enthusiasts, body composition goals, sustainability-focused lifters

Philosophy: Strength on primary lifts meets meaningful hypertrophy on secondary/accessory work. Leads to muscular, capable athletes.

3. SBD Full Power (Adjustable 12–16 Weeks)

Focus: Compete in all three lifts (squat, bench, deadlift)

Best for: Full powerlifting meets, well-rounded strength athletes

Philosophy: Rotates through focused blocks for each lift while maintaining baseline competency in others. Meet prep for the complete package.

Real-World Example: SBD Meet Prep (16-Week Timeline)

Athlete Profile

Marcus, raw powerlifter: Bench 405, Squat 500, Deadlift 600. Total: 1505 lbs. Meet in 16 weeks.

Week 1–4: Squat Block (Skill)

Focus: Squat technique and positioning. Bench and deadlift maintained at moderate volume.

  • Main: Squat 3×5 @ RPE 6–7
  • Secondary: Box Squat (weak-point-specific) 3×5 @ RPE 6.5
  • Bench: 2×4 @ RPE 6 (maintenance)
  • Deadlift: 1×5 @ RPE 6 (maintenance)

Week 5–8: Bench Block (Strength)

Focus: Bench press strength and volume. Squat and deadlift maintained.

  • Main: Bench 3×4 @ RPE 7–8
  • Secondary: Incline Bench (weak-point-specific) 3×4 @ RPE 7.5
  • Squat: 2×3 @ RPE 6.5 (maintenance)
  • Deadlift: 1×3 @ RPE 6.5 (maintenance)

Week 9–12: Deadlift Block (Peak)

Focus: Deadlift intensity and competition readiness. Squat and bench taper slightly.

  • Main: Deadlift 2×3, 1×1 @ RPE 8–9
  • Secondary: Speed Deadlift 3×2 @ RPE 7
  • Squat: 2×2 @ RPE 7 (taper)
  • Bench: 2×2 @ RPE 7 (taper)

Week 13–14: Deload + Final Taper

Recovery week. All RPE 5–6, reduced volume. Prepare body for opener attempts.

Week 15–16: Meet Week

Opener attempts on all three lifts. Attempt selection based on readiness and recent performance.

Result

Marcus arrives at the meet with a balanced, periodized build: Squat +20 lbs (520), Bench +15 lbs (420), Deadlift +25 lbs (625). Total: 1565 lbs (+60 lbs from baseline).

How to Choose Your Path

Choose Bench Specialization If:

  • You compete (or want to compete) in bench-only competitions
  • Your primary goal is a bigger bench press
  • You have limited training time (3 days/week) and want to maximize bench progress
  • You're willing to sacrifice squat/deadlift development for bench gains

Choose Jacked & Strong If:

  • You want balanced strength + muscle growth across all lifts
  • You prioritize body composition and aesthetics alongside strength
  • You enjoy variety in exercises and don't have a specific meet goal
  • You want a sustainable, long-term training path without peaking

Choose SBD Full Power If:

  • You compete in full powerlifting (or want to prepare for it)
  • You want competitive totals in all three lifts
  • You like periodized blocks with clear phases for each lift
  • You have 4–5 days per week to train

Switching Programs Mid-Cycle

You can switch programs at block gates (end of week 4, 8, 12). When you switch:

  • Current 1RMs are preserved: Your estimated maxes carry over, so load calculations continue logically
  • Block progression resets: You start the new program at Block 1 (Skill) of the new architecture
  • Weak point re-assessment is recommended: Different programs have different weak-point routing, so reassess if switching to SBD
Example: You finish a Bench Specialization cycle (Bench +25 lbs). You want to try Jacked & Strong. At the block gate, you switch. Your bench 1RM carries over, and you begin Jacked & Strong Block 1 with new exercise variety and higher accessory volume.

Key Takeaways

  • Three program architectures: Bench Spec (bench-focused), Jacked & Strong (balanced), SBD (full powerlifting)
  • Each program uses the same adaptive engines (load, readiness, weak point, joint health)
  • Choose based on your goals, training frequency, and competition style
  • Switch programs at block gates without losing progress
  • All programs include autoregulation, joint monitoring, and intelligent adaptation
